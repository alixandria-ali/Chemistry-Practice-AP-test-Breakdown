# Question 11: pH and Hydrogen Ion Concentration

## Unit: Unit 8 - Acids and Bases
**Topic**: pH Scale and Calculations (SAP-9.A, SAP-9.B)

---

## Original Question
A solution has a pH of 4.20. Using the relationship between pH and pOH, what is the concentration of OH⁻?

A. 9.9 × 10⁻⁵ M
B. 6.2 × 10⁻⁵ M
C. 6.3 × 10⁻⁵ M
D. 6.7 × 10⁻⁶ M
E. 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ M

---

## How to Approach This Question

### Step 1: Understand the pH-pOH Relationship
At 25°C:
- **pH + pOH = 14.00**
- **pH** = -log[H⁺]
- **pOH** = -log[OH⁻]
- **[H⁺] × [OH⁻] = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴** (Kw, ion product constant)

### Step 2: Calculate pOH from pH
Given: pH = 4.20

pOH = 14.00 - pH
pOH = 14.00 - 4.20
pOH = **9.80**

### Step 3: Calculate [OH⁻] from pOH
[OH⁻] = 10⁻ᵖᴼᴴ
[OH⁻] = 10⁻⁹·⁸⁰

**Calculate:**
10⁻⁹·⁸⁰ = 10⁻⁹ × 10⁻⁰·⁸⁰
10⁻⁰·⁸⁰ ≈ 0.158
10⁻⁹·⁸⁰ ≈ 1.58 × 10⁻¹⁰

**Answer: E (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ M)**

---

## Alternative Method: Using Kw Directly

### Step 1: Find [H⁺] from pH
[H⁺] = 10⁻ᵖᴴ
[H⁺] = 10⁻⁴·²⁰
[H⁺] ≈ 6.3 × 10⁻⁵ M

### Step 2: Use Kw to Find [OH⁻]
Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴

[OH⁻] = Kw / [H⁺]
[OH⁻] = (1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴) / (6.3 × 10⁻⁵)
[OH⁻] = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ M ✓

---

## Key Concepts to Remember

### pH Scale Relationships:
- **pH = -log[H⁺]** → [H⁺] = 10⁻ᵖᴴ
- **pOH = -log[OH⁻]** → [OH⁻] = 10⁻ᵖᴼᴴ
- **pH + pOH = 14.00** (at 25°C)
- **[H⁺][OH⁻] = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴ = Kw** (at 25°C)

### pH Scale:
- **pH < 7**: Acidic ([H⁺] > [OH⁻])
- **pH = 7**: Neutral ([H⁺] = [OH⁻] = 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ M)
- **pH > 7**: Basic ([H⁺] < [OH⁻])

### Inverse Relationship:
- As pH ↑, [H⁺] ↓ and [OH⁻] ↑
- As pH ↓, [H⁺] ↑ and [OH⁻] ↓

To calculate 10⁻ˣ:
1. Enter the exponent (as negative)
2. Press "10ˣ" or "2nd" + "log"

## Try It Now!

**Practice Question:**

A solution has a pOH of 3.50. What is the pH of the solution, and what is the concentration of H⁺ ions?

A. pH = 10.50, [H⁺] = 3.2 × 10⁻¹¹ M
B. pH = 10.50, [H⁺] = 3.2 × 10⁻⁴ M
C. pH = 3.50, [H⁺] = 3.2 × 10⁻⁴ M
D. pH = 7.00, [H⁺] = 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ M
E. pH = 10.50, [H⁺] = 3.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ M

---

## Check Yourself

### Your Answer: ____

### Feedback

**Approach:**
1. Use pH + pOH = 14 to find pH
2. Use [H⁺] = 10⁻ᵖᴴ to find hydrogen ion concentration

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1: Calculate pH**
pH + pOH = 14.00
pH = 14.00 - pOH
pH = 14.00 - 3.50
pH = **10.50**

**Step 2: Calculate [H⁺]**
[H⁺] = 10⁻ᵖᴴ
[H⁺] = 10⁻¹⁰·⁵⁰
[H⁺] = 10⁻¹⁰ × 10⁻⁰·⁵⁰
[H⁺] = 10⁻¹⁰ × 0.316
[H⁺] = **3.16 × 10⁻¹¹ M** ≈ 3.2 × 10⁻¹¹ M

**Correct Answer: A**

The solution is **basic** (pH > 7), with a low concentration of hydrogen ions. A pOH of 3.50 indicates a relatively high concentration of OH⁻ ions (about 3.2 × 10⁻⁴ M), which makes sense for a basic solution.

**Verification:**
Check using Kw:
- [OH⁻] = 10⁻³·⁵⁰ ≈ 3.2 × 10⁻⁴ M
- [H⁺] × [OH⁻] = (3.2 × 10⁻¹¹)(3.2 × 10⁻⁴) ≈ 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴ ✓

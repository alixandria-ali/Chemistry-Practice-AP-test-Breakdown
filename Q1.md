# Question 1: Pi Bond Formation

## Unit: Unit 2 - Molecular and Ionic Compound Structure and Properties
**Topic**: Covalent Bonding and Orbital Overlap (SAP-3.A)

---

## Original Question
Which orbitals can overlap to form a pi (π) bond?
- A. two p orbitals
- B. one s orbital and one p orbital
- C. one s orbital and one hybrid orbital
- D. two hybrid orbitals
- E. two s orbitals

---

## How to Approach This Question

### Step 1: Understand Pi Bond Formation
A **pi (π) bond** forms from **side-by-side overlap** of atomic orbitals (not head-on overlap like sigma bonds).

### Step 2: Identify Which Orbitals Can Overlap Side-by-Side
- **p orbitals** have two lobes oriented along an axis
- When two p orbitals approach parallel to each other, their lobes can overlap sideways
- This creates electron density **above and below** the internuclear axis

### Step 3: Eliminate Wrong Answers
- **s orbitals** are spherical → can only overlap head-on (sigma bonds only)
- **hybrid orbitals** (sp, sp², sp³) are designed for head-on overlap → sigma bonds only
- **s and p combinations** would overlap head-on → sigma bonds only

### Step 4: Select the Correct Answer
Only **two p orbitals** can provide the parallel, side-by-side overlap needed for a pi bond.

**Answer: A**

---

## Key Concepts to Remember

1. **Sigma (σ) bonds**: Head-on overlap (s-s, s-p, p-p head-on, hybrid-hybrid, hybrid-s, hybrid-p)
2. **Pi (π) bonds**: Side-by-side overlap (p-p parallel only)
3. **Multiple bonds**: 
   - Double bond = 1 σ + 1 π
   - Triple bond = 1 σ + 2 π
4. **Unhybridized p orbitals** are essential for pi bonding

## Try It Now!

**Practice Question:**

In the molecule ethene (C₂H₄), which has a carbon-carbon double bond, how many pi bonds are present and what type of orbital overlap creates them?

A. One pi bond formed by sp²-sp² overlap
B. One pi bond formed by p-p overlap
C. Two pi bonds formed by p-p overlap
D. One pi bond formed by sp²-p overlap
E. No pi bonds are present

---

## Check Yourself

### Your Answer: ____

### Feedback

**Approach:**
1. Ethene has a **C=C double bond**
2. Each carbon is **sp² hybridized** (to form 3 sigma bonds: 1 to the other C, 2 to H atoms)
3. Each carbon has **one unhybridized p orbital** remaining
4. These two unhybridized p orbitals overlap **side-by-side** to form the pi bond
5. A double bond = 1 sigma + 1 pi bond

**Correct Answer: B**

One pi bond is formed by the side-by-side overlap of the unhybridized p orbital from each carbon atom. The sp² hybrid orbitals form sigma bonds, but only unhybridized p orbitals can form pi bonds.

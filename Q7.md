# Question 7: Molarity Calculation

## Unit: Unit 3 - Intermolecular Forces and Properties / Unit 4 - Chemical Reactions
**Topic**: Molarity and Solution Concentration (SPQ-3.A)

---

## Original Question
Isopropanol (C₃H₈O) is a key ingredient in some hand sanitizers. Suppose that 127 grams of isopropanol is dissolved in water. The volume of the solution is 1.250 milliliters. What is the molarity of the solution?

Refer to the periodic table to help you answer. Express your answer to three significant figures.

The molarity of the solution is ____ M.

---

## How to Approach This Question

### Step 1: Understand Molarity
**Molarity (M)** = moles of solute / liters of solution

### Step 2: Convert Grams of Solute to Moles

**Find molar mass of C₃H₈O:**
- C: 3 × 12.01 g/mol = 36.03 g/mol
- H: 8 × 1.008 g/mol = 8.064 g/mol
- O: 1 × 16.00 g/mol = 16.00 g/mol
- **Total: 60.09 g/mol**

**Calculate moles:**
moles = mass / molar mass
moles = 127 g / 60.09 g/mol
moles = **2.113 mol**

### Step 3: Convert mL to L
Volume = 1.250 mL = 1.250 mL × (1 L / 1000 mL) = **0.001250 L**

### Step 4: Calculate Molarity
M = moles / liters
M = 2.113 mol / 0.001250 L
M = **1690 M** (to 3 significant figures: **1.69 × 10³ M**)

Wait - this seems unrealistically high! Let me check if there's a typo in the problem.

**Note:** The problem likely meant 1.250 L (or 1250 mL), not 1.250 mL, which would give:
M = 2.113 mol / 1.250 L = **1.69 M** ✓

**Answer: 1.69 M** (assuming 1.250 L solution volume)

---

## Key Concepts to Remember

### Molarity Formula:
**M = n / V**
- M = molarity (mol/L)
- n = moles of solute
- V = volume of solution in liters

### Conversion Steps:
1. **Mass → Moles**: divide by molar mass
   - moles = grams / (g/mol)

2. **mL → L**: divide by 1000
   - L = mL / 1000

3. **Calculate M**: divide moles by liters
   - M = moles / L

### Common Units:
- Molarity: M or mol/L
- Volume: L (must convert from mL)
- Amount: moles
- Mass: grams

A. 0.250 M
B. 0.500 M
C. 0.750 M
D. 1.00 M
E. 2.00 M

### Feedback

**Approach:**

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1: Convert grams to moles**
moles = mass / molar mass
moles = 45.0 g / 180.16 g/mol

**Step 2: Convert mL to L**
V = 500.0 mL × (1 L / 1000 mL) = 0.5000 L

**Step 3: Calculate molarity**
M = moles / liters
M = 0.2498 mol / 0.5000 L
M = 0.500 M (rounded to 3 sig figs)

**Correct Answer: B (0.500 M)**

The molarity is 0.500 M. This means there are 0.500 moles of glucose per liter of solution. This is a typical concentration for laboratory solutions.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Forgetting to convert mL to L (always divide by 1000!)
- Using mass instead of moles in the molarity formula
- Calculating molar mass incorrectly
- Not matching significant figures to the given data

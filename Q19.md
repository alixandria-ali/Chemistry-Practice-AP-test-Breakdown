# Question 19: Gas Stoichiometry with Ideal Gas Law

## Unit: Unit 4 - Chemical Reactions / Unit 5 - Kinetics and Thermodynamics
**Topic**: Stoichiometry Combined with Gas Laws (SPQ-4.B, SAP-7.A)

---

## Original Question
When magnesium reacts with hydrochloric acid, hydrogen gas is formed:
2HCl + Mg → H₂ + MgCl₂

What is the volume of hydrogen produced at 25°C and 101.3 kilopascals when 49.0 grams of HCl reacts with excess magnesium? Use the periodic table and ideal gas resource.

A. 1.38 L
B. 2.76 L
C. 16.4 L
D. 32.9 L
E. 33.1 L

---

## How to Approach This Question

This problem combines **stoichiometry** with the **ideal gas law**. We need to:
1. Convert mass of reactant to moles
2. Use stoichiometry to find moles of gas product
3. Use ideal gas law to find volume

---

### Step 1: Convert Mass of HCl to Moles

**Molar mass of HCl:**
- H: 1.008 g/mol
- Cl: 35.45 g/mol
- **Total: 36.46 g/mol**

**Convert to moles:**
moles HCl = 49.0 g / 36.46 g/mol = **1.344 mol**

---

### Step 2: Use Stoichiometry to Find Moles of H₂

From balanced equation: **2HCl + Mg → H₂ + MgCl₂**

**Mole ratio**: 2 mol HCl : 1 mol H₂

moles H₂ = 1.344 mol HCl × (1 mol H₂ / 2 mol HCl)
moles H₂ = **0.672 mol**

---

### Step 3: Use Ideal Gas Law to Find Volume

**Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT**

Where:
- P = pressure (in kPa)
- V = volume (in L) ← what we're solving for
- n = moles
- R = gas constant = **8.314 L·kPa/(mol·K)**
- T = temperature (in Kelvin)

**Convert temperature to Kelvin:**
T = 25°C + 273.15 = **298.15 K** (or just use 298 K)

**Solve for V:**
V = nRT / P

V = (0.672 mol)(8.314 L·kPa/(mol·K))(298 K) / (101.3 kPa)

V = 1663.7 / 101.3

V = **16.42 L** ≈ **16.4 L**

**Answer: C (16.4 L)**

---

## Key Concepts to Remember

### Gas Stoichiometry Process:
```
Mass reactant → Moles reactant → Moles gas → Volume gas
   (÷ MM)       (× mole ratio)      (PV=nRT)
```

### Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT

**Choose the correct R value** based on units:
- R = 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K) when P is in atm
- R = 8.314 L·kPa/(mol·K) when P is in kPa
- R = 62.4 L·mmHg/(mol·K) when P is in mmHg

### Temperature Conversion:
**Always convert to Kelvin!**
- K = °C + 273.15
- Never use Celsius in gas law calculations

### Common Mistakes:
- Forgetting to convert °C to K
- Using wrong R value for pressure units
- Forgetting to use stoichiometry ratio
- Using mass instead of moles

## Try It Now!

**Practice Question:**

Zinc reacts with sulfuric acid to produce hydrogen gas:
Zn + H₂SO₄ → H₂ + ZnSO₄

If 32.7 g of zinc reacts with excess sulfuric acid, what volume of hydrogen gas is produced at 20°C and 1.00 atm?

(Molar mass of Zn = 65.38 g/mol; R = 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K))

A. 6.00 L
B. 12.0 L
C. 24.0 L
D. 48.0 L
E. 96.0 L

---

## Check Yourself

### Your Answer: ____

### Feedback

**Approach:**
1. Convert mass of Zn to moles
2. Use 1:1 mole ratio to find moles of H₂
3. Use PV = nRT to find volume

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1: Convert grams of Zn to moles**
moles Zn = 32.7 g / 65.38 g/mol = 0.500 mol

**Step 2: Use stoichiometry**
From equation: 1 mol Zn : 1 mol H₂
moles H₂ = 0.500 mol × (1 mol H₂ / 1 mol Zn) = 0.500 mol

**Step 3: Convert temperature**
T = 20°C + 273 = 293 K

**Step 4: Use ideal gas law**
PV = nRT
V = nRT / P
V = (0.500 mol)(0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K))(293 K) / (1.00 atm)
V = 12.03 / 1.00
V = **12.0 L**

**Correct Answer: B (12.0 L)**

**Verification at STP:**
At standard conditions (0°C, 1 atm), 1 mole of gas occupies 22.4 L.
We have 0.500 mol at slightly higher temperature (20°C vs 0°C) and same pressure.
Expected volume: 0.500 × 22.4 × (293/273) ≈ 12.0 L ✓

**Key Insight:**
The 1:1 stoichiometric ratio makes this calculation simpler - the moles of Zn equal the moles of H₂. However, always check the balanced equation as ratios vary!

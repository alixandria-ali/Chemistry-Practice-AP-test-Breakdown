# AP Chemistry Practice Exam #1 - Complete Breakdown

**Section II: Free Response Questions (7 Questions)**

This site provides comprehensive breakdowns with solutions, explanations, and practice questions for AP Chemistry Practice Exam #1 - Section II.

---

## Test Overview

- **Format**: 7 Free-Response Questions
- **Time**: 1 hour 45 minutes
- **Weight**: 50% of total exam score
- **Topics**: All AP Chemistry units with focus on thermodynamics, kinetics, equilibrium, acids/bases, electrochemistry

---

## 📖 Questions

### Long Free-Response Questions (10 points each, ~23 minutes)

1. **[Question 1: Ethanol Combustion](Q1.html)**
   - Thermochemistry, stoichiometry, oxidation-reduction, intermolecular forces, equilibrium
   - Topics: Redox reactions, gas laws, heat calculations, hydrogen bonding, Le Chatelier's Principle

2. **[Question 2: Hydrogen Peroxide Decomposition](Q2.html)**
   - Particulate representations, energy diagrams, catalysis, kinetics
   - Topics: Molecular diagrams, activation energy, catalyst effects, reaction rates, balanced equations

3. **[Question 3: Sulfur Trioxide](Q3.html)**
   - Lewis structures, VSEPR theory, thermodynamics, equilibrium
   - Topics: Molecular geometry, Gibbs free energy, Kp calculations, Le Chatelier's Principle

### Short Free-Response Questions (4 points each, ~9 minutes)

4. **[Question 4: Hypochlorous Acid Equilibrium](Q4.html)**
   - Acid-base equilibria, titration curves, buffers
   - Topics: Conjugate pairs, weak vs strong acids, buffer requirements

5. **[Question 5: NADH Electrochemistry](Q5.html)**
   - Electrochemical cells, Gibbs free energy, redox reactions
   - Topics: Cell potentials, electron transfer, Nernst equation, ΔG° calculations

6. **[Question 6: Paraffin Wax Phase Changes](Q6.html)**
   - Thermal energy, heat of fusion, particle-level explanations
   - Topics: Endothermic processes, phase transitions, density, molecular spacing

7. **[Question 7: Phenol Red Spectrophotometry](Q7.html)**
   - pH calculations, Beer's Law, dilution, spectroscopy
   - Topics: pH/[H⁺] conversions, calibration curves, absorbance, wavelength effects

## Study Tips for AP Chemistry FRQs

### Time Management
- Long FRQs (1-3): Spend approximately 23 minutes each
- Short FRQs (4-7): Spend approximately 9 minutes each
- Leave time to review your work

### Earning Points
- **Show your work**: Write out equations and calculations clearly
- **Include units**: Always include proper units in your answers
- **Justify answers**: Explain your reasoning when asked
- **Use significant figures**: Pay attention to sig figs in calculations
- **Label clearly**: Make it easy for graders to follow your logic

### Common Point-Earning Opportunities
- Correctly identifying trends and patterns
- Providing particle-level explanations
- Connecting macroscopic observations to molecular behavior
- Using proper chemical nomenclature and notation
- Drawing accurate diagrams and structures

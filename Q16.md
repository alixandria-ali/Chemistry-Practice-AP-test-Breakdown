# Question 16: Stoichiometry - Mass to Mass Calculation

## Unit: Unit 4 - Chemical Reactions
**Topic**: Stoichiometry and Mass Relationships (SPQ-4.A, SPQ-4.B)

---

## Original Question
Sodium phosphate and calcium chloride react to form sodium chloride and calcium phosphate. If you have 379.4 grams of calcium chloride and an excess of sodium phosphate, how much calcium phosphate can you make? Use the periodic table and the polyatomic ion resource.

A. 353.5 g
B. 379.4 g
C. 402.2 g
D. 863.4 g
E. 1,060 g

---

## How to Approach This Question

### Step 1: Write the Balanced Equation

**Identify the reactants and products:**
- Reactants: Sodium phosphate (Na₃PO₄) + Calcium chloride (CaCl₂)
- Products: Sodium chloride (NaCl) + Calcium phosphate (Ca₃(PO₄)₂)

**Write the unbalanced equation:**
Na₃PO₄ + CaCl₂ → NaCl + Ca₃(PO₄)₂

**Balance the equation:**
- Ca: Need 3 on left → 3CaCl₂
- PO₄: Need 2 on left → 2Na₃PO₄
- Na: Have 6, need 6 on right → 6NaCl
- Cl: Have 6, get 6 → balanced ✓

**Balanced equation:**
**2Na₃PO₄ + 3CaCl₂ → 6NaCl + Ca₃(PO₄)₂**

---

### Step 2: Convert Mass of CaCl₂ to Moles

**Calculate molar mass of CaCl₂:**
- Ca: 1 × 40.08 g/mol = 40.08 g/mol
- Cl: 2 × 35.45 g/mol = 70.90 g/mol
- **Total: 110.98 g/mol**

**Convert to moles:**
moles CaCl₂ = mass / molar mass
moles CaCl₂ = 379.4 g / 110.98 g/mol
moles CaCl₂ = **3.418 mol**

---

### Step 3: Use Mole Ratio to Find Moles of Ca₃(PO₄)₂

From balanced equation: **3 mol CaCl₂ : 1 mol Ca₃(PO₄)₂**

moles Ca₃(PO₄)₂ = 3.418 mol CaCl₂ × (1 mol Ca₃(PO₄)₂ / 3 mol CaCl₂)
moles Ca₃(PO₄)₂ = **1.139 mol**

---

### Step 4: Convert Moles of Ca₃(PO₄)₂ to Mass

**Calculate molar mass of Ca₃(PO₄)₂:**
- Ca: 3 × 40.08 g/mol = 120.24 g/mol
- P: 2 × 30.97 g/mol = 61.94 g/mol
- O: 8 × 16.00 g/mol = 128.00 g/mol
- **Total: 310.18 g/mol**

**Convert to mass:**
mass = moles × molar mass
mass = 1.139 mol × 310.18 g/mol
mass = **353.3 g** ≈ **353.5 g**

**Answer: A (353.5 g)**

---

## Key Concepts to Remember

### Stoichiometry Road Map:
```
Mass A → Moles A → Moles B → Mass B
  (÷MM)    (× ratio)   (× MM)
```

### Step-by-Step Process:
1. **Write and balance** the chemical equation
2. **Convert given mass to moles**: moles = mass / molar mass
3. **Use mole ratio** from balanced equation
4. **Convert moles to mass**: mass = moles × molar mass

### Important Notes:
- Always work with **balanced equations**
- Mole ratios come from **coefficients** in balanced equation
- "Excess" means you don't need to worry about that reactant limiting
- Keep extra digits during calculation, round at the end

## Try It Now!

**Practice Question:**

Aluminum reacts with oxygen to form aluminum oxide according to the equation:
4Al + 3O₂ → 2Al₂O₃

If 54.0 grams of aluminum react with excess oxygen, how many grams of aluminum oxide are produced?

(Molar masses: Al = 26.98 g/mol, Al₂O₃ = 101.96 g/mol)

A. 50.0 g
B. 54.0 g
C. 102 g
D. 204 g
E. 408 g

---

## Check Yourself

### Your Answer: ____

### Feedback

**Approach:**
Use the stoichiometry road map: Mass Al → Moles Al → Moles Al₂O₃ → Mass Al₂O₃

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1: Balanced equation is given**
4Al + 3O₂ → 2Al₂O₃

**Step 2: Convert mass of Al to moles**
moles Al = 54.0 g / 26.98 g/mol = 2.00 mol Al

**Step 3: Use mole ratio to find moles of Al₂O₃**
From equation: 4 mol Al : 2 mol Al₂O₃ (or 2:1 simplified)

moles Al₂O₃ = 2.00 mol Al × (2 mol Al₂O₃ / 4 mol Al)
moles Al₂O₃ = 2.00 mol × 0.5 = 1.00 mol Al₂O₃

**Step 4: Convert moles of Al₂O₃ to mass**
mass Al₂O₃ = 1.00 mol × 101.96 g/mol = 102 g

**Correct Answer: C (102 g)**

Notice that the mass of aluminum oxide produced (102 g) is greater than the mass of aluminum we started with (54.0 g) because oxygen atoms have been added. This is expected based on the law of conservation of mass - the oxygen contributes mass to the product.

**Key Insight:**
The 4:2 ratio means that 4 moles of Al produce 2 moles of Al₂O₃, or simplified, 2 moles of Al produce 1 mole of Al₂O₃. Don't forget to simplify ratios to make calculations easier!
